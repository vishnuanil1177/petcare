Training tips

  • Use positive reinforcement: Reward good behavior with treats, praise, or a clicker.
  • Teach basic commands (sit, stay, come) through repetition and treats.
  • Practice controlled, positive leash walking; reward loose-leash behavior.
  • Gradually introduce your dog to new environments, people, and animals.
  • Make the crate a comfortable space; acclimate your dog with rewards.
  • Provide appropriate chew toys; redirect from unwanted items.
  • Practice Patience: Allow your dog time to learn and avoid getting frustrated during training.
  • Use Clear Signals: Be consistent in your cues and gestures to avoid confusion.
  • Utilize High-Value Treats: Use treats that your dog finds especially rewarding for better motivation.
  • Offer Verbal Praise: Use a positive and encouraging tone to reinforce good behavior.
  • End on a Positive Note: Conclude sessions with a positive experience to keep training enjoyable.

Vaccination details

  • Distemper: Protects against a contagious and often fatal virus affecting a dog's respiratory, gastrointestinal, and nervous systems.
  • Parvovirus: Guards against a highly contagious virus causing severe gastrointestinal issues, particularly in puppies.
  • Canine Hepatitis: Provides immunity against a virus affecting the liver, kidneys, spleen, and lungs.
  • Rabies: Mandatory vaccination protecting against a fatal virus that affects the nervous system.
  • Kennel Cough (Bordetella): Recommended for dogs in social environments, boarding facilities, or group settings.
  • Leptospirosis: Advisable for dogs exposed to wildlife, water sources, or urban areas.
  • Puppy Vaccinations: Typically start at 6-8 weeks, with boosters every 3-4 weeks until around 16 weeks.
  • Adult Dogs: Follow-up boosters annually or as advised by a veterinarian based on the dog's health and risk factors.

Pet Grooming

  • Regular Brushing: Brush your dog's coat frequently to remove loose hair and prevent matting.
  • Bath Frequency: Bathe your dog as needed, using dog-specific shampoo to avoid skin irritation.
  • Ear Cleaning: Gently clean your dog's ears to prevent infections, using a vet-approved cleaner.
  • Nail Trimming: Keep your dog's nails trimmed to a comfortable length to prevent overgrowth.
  • Teeth Care: Brush your dog's teeth regularly or use dental treats to maintain oral hygiene.
  • Eye Care: Check and clean your dog's eyes to prevent dirt buildup or irritation.
  • Fur Inspection: Regularly inspect your dog's skin and coat for any signs of ticks, fleas, or abnormalities.
  • Professional Grooming: Consider professional grooming sessions for specific breeds or complex grooming needs.
  • Specialized Care: Certain breeds or coat types may require specific grooming techniques or tools.
